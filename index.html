How it Works

The Leaky Bucket Algorithm is a congestion control mechanism used in networks to shape traffic. It ensures that data is transmitted at a constant, predictable rate, regardless of the burstiness of incoming traffic.

Imagine a bucket with a small hole at the bottom. Water (packets) enters the bucket at varying rates. If the water enters faster than it can leak out, the bucket fills up. If the bucket becomes full, any additional water overflows (packets are dropped). The water always leaves the bucket at a constant rate through the hole.

Advantages

  • Smooths out bursty traffic.
  • Ensures a constant output rate.
  • Prevents network congestion.

Limitations

  • Packets are lost if the bucket overflows.
  • Does not allow for bursts even if bandwidth is available.
